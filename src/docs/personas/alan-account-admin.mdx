In small operations, this person signed up for Arda, created the initial item
catalog, and invited the team. They often wear a second hat as the Inventory
Manager (Arda Manager). In medium to large operations, it may be a dedicated IT
coordinator or operations manager with admin-level access. Distinguished from
the <a href="?path=/docs/docs-personas-owen-business-principal--docs">Business
Principal (Owen)</a> by focus: Owen owns the business case and strategic
direction; Alan owns the platform, the onboarding journey, and reference data
quality.

**Responsibilities:** Setting up and configuring the Arda account, managing user
accounts and permissions, maintaining company information (legal name, address,
tax IDs, Registration ID, NAICS codes), overseeing subscription and billing, and
ensuring the system meets compliance requirements. Responsible for the initial
organization setup: creating the account, following the &#8220;Get Started with
Arda&#8221; onboarding panel (set up company, add inventory items, invite team).
Owns the **onboarding journey** for every new user and every new inventory area
brought under management. Owns **reference data stewardship** &#8212; ensuring
the item catalog, vendor information, and card configurations are accurate and
complete enough for the operational personas to do their work.

## Goals and Motivations

- Keep the Arda account configured correctly so the operational personas can do
  their jobs without friction.
- Workers can sign in without issues. Company information is accurate on purchase
  orders. New hires get access within their first day.
- Set it up correctly once and not have to think about it. Reactive account
  management is the worst outcome.
- **Get new users productive fast.** Guide each persona through their first
  session: first sign-in, the onboarding panel, their first scan or order.
- **Keep reference data clean and complete.** Every item has a supplier, a
  reorder quantity, and a valid ordering link before it reaches David&#8217;s
  queue. Incomplete data creates friction downstream; Alan prevents it upstream.
- Maintaining compliance with healthcare industry regulations (accurate company
  records, audit trails).
- Seeing measurable results: fewer emergency orders, less waste, less downtime,
  reduced stress for the team.

## Challenges and Pain Points

- **Onboarding new team members** requires setting up accounts, assigning roles,
  and providing persona-specific training. Sam needs to know how to scan; David
  needs the queue; Keisha needs the receiving flow.
- **Onboarding new inventory areas** is the larger challenge. Importing items,
  setting up supplier references, configuring reorder quantities, and preparing
  the first batch of cards &#8212; all before the team starts using the new area.
- **Deferred validation:** Initial data imports produce items with incomplete or
  guessed values. Alan must accept imperfect records, mark them for later review,
  and systematically validate them without blocking the team from starting.
- **Pre-print review:** Before printing the first batch of cards, Alan must
  review critical fields (item name, reorder quantity, supplier, SKU) in a table
  view. Cards with wrong data erode trust and waste laminating supplies.
- **Vendor data completeness:** Items imported from order history may lack a
  supplier URL, order method, or SKU. These gaps must be filled during data
  cleanup, not discovered one by one in the order queue.
- **Filtering and tracking during rollout:** Filter by vendor, hide already-
  configured items, track which items are ready vs. still need attention.
- Company information changes must be propagated accurately for compliance and
  PO generation.
- **Settings page complexity:** Managing multiple settings pages &#8212; the
  Settings hub (Account, Companies, Appearance, Notifications, Display tabs),
  Company Settings (Company Info, Users, Subscription, Billing tabs), and
  Account Profile &#8212; with related but distinct functionality.
- No dedicated admin dashboard for monitoring system health and user activity.
- Infrequent use of admin screens means re-orientation each time.

### Fears and Objections

- Accidentally changing a setting that affects all users system-wide.
- Billing surprises from subscription tier changes.
- Security concerns about user access and password policies (AWS Cognito-based
  auth).
- A user unable to sign in calling during a busy day, or service interruption
  due to an expired payment method.
- Printing a batch of cards with bad data and having to redo them &#8212;
  destroying team confidence in the system.
- The initial data import being so messy that cleanup takes longer than manual
  entry would have.

## Typical Day

1. **Weekly:** Review company settings, check for pending user invitations or
   access requests.
2. **Monthly:** Review subscription status, check billing, update company
   information if needed.
3. **Ad hoc:** Onboard new team members (Company Settings &#8594; Users, invite
   with role, guide through signup and the &#8220;Get Started with Arda&#8221;
   onboarding panel). Troubleshoot user issues. Adjust notification preferences.
   Set up Quickstart Kit hardware (HP 9135E dual-tray printer, laminator,
   scanner) for the team.
4. **During rollout phases:** Import items from vendor order history. Review and
   clean up imported data: fill in missing suppliers, correct reorder quantities,
   assign item types and locations. Filter by vendor to work systematically.
   Review critical fields before printing the first batch of cards. Track which
   items are printed, which still need review, and which are ready to go live.
5. **Occasionally:** Check dashboard KPIs (Total Orders, Orders Placed, Growth
   Rate) to understand platform usage and order volumes.
6. **Strategically:** Coordinate with
   <a href="?path=/docs/docs-personas-owen-business-principal--docs">Owen</a>
   on which inventory areas to bring under management next. Plan the data
   preparation and user training needed for each expansion phase.

**Session pattern:** Infrequent, purposeful sessions for account administration.
During rollout and onboarding phases, sessions become longer and more
frequent &#8212; potentially daily 30&#8211;60 minute data cleanup sessions when
importing a new inventory area. Not a daily user in the Admin role outside of
rollout phases, though they may use Arda daily in a secondary role (e.g., as
Inventory Manager / Arda Manager).

## Technology and Environment

- Office desktop or laptop, dedicated workstation (not shared).
- Full browser access (Chrome, 1920&#215;1080).
- High technical proficiency relative to other Arda users.
- Comfortable with SaaS administration consoles, multi-tab workflows, and
  settings interfaces.
- During data cleanup, may have a spreadsheet open alongside Arda for
  cross-referencing vendor order history.
- No scanning or printing requirements in the Admin role.

## Decision-Making Process

Makes system configuration decisions independently. Consults with
<a href="?path=/docs/docs-personas-owen-business-principal--docs">Owen</a> for
subscription and billing changes and for strategic rollout timing. Coordinates
with the Inventory Manager on workflow-affecting settings and data quality
standards. May delegate day-to-day operational decisions entirely.

## Communication Preferences

- **Email** for formal changes and audit trails.
- **In-app notifications** for system alerts.
- Prefers structured settings pages over conversational interfaces.
- Coordinates with operational staff (especially the Inventory Manager) about
  any configuration changes that affect workflows.
- Creates onboarding checklists for new team members and new inventory areas.

## Personality

Organized, cautious about changes, thorough in documentation. Prefers clear
confirmation before applying system-wide settings. Wants structured pages with
clear descriptions of what each option controls. Patient with data
cleanup &#8212; understands that good data now prevents problems later. Takes
pride in a clean, complete item catalog. Creates onboarding checklists for new
team members and new inventory areas.
